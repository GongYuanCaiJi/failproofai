Quick answers to the questions teams ask most when evaluating AgentEye. For the vocabulary used below, see [Concepts](/agenteye/concepts).

---

## Is AgentEye self-hosted? Does my data leave my environment?

Yes, it's self-hosted, and no. You run the server, dashboard, and databases in your own infrastructure. Events, prompts, model responses, and analytics stay in storage you control. Even in the [Managed](/agenteye/managed-deployment) option, your data lives in your own cloud account. See [Security](/agenteye/security).

## Which databases do I need?

Two: **ClickHouse** (required: it stores all events and analytics) and **PostgreSQL** 15+ (required: it stores organizations, users, API keys, dashboards, and saved queries). **Redis** is optional; it's a cache and rate-limit backend, and everything degrades gracefully without it.

## What does AgentEye instrument?

Your agent code, through the [Python SDK](/agenteye/python-sdk). You add `agenteye.event.*` calls at the points you care about: agent start/end, model requests and responses, tool calls, hooks, errors, and human-in-the-loop steps. Instrumentation is explicit, so you decide exactly what is recorded.

## Do I have to score my runs? What's an "evaluator"?

Scoring is opt-in. Sessions are always recorded, but they're only scored once you connect an **evaluator**, a small scoring service you run that AgentEye POSTs each finished run to. AgentEye doesn't ship a default evaluator; the scoring logic is yours. Until you connect one, sessions simply appear unscored. See [Evaluation suite](/agenteye/evaluation-suite).

## What's the difference between alerts and audits?

An **alert** watches a metric you already know about (error rate, latency, cost, an evaluator score) and pages you when it crosses a threshold. An **audit** is the other direction: a recurring investigation that mines your logs across sessions for failure patterns you *haven't* written a rule for, and hands you ranked, evidence-backed findings. Use alerts for known risks and audits to discover unknown ones. See [Alerts](/agenteye/alerts) and [Audits](/agenteye/audits).

## Can multiple teams share one instance?

Yes. One AgentEye instance can host many **organizations**, each isolated at the database layer with its own users, keys, and data. See [Tenant management](/agenteye/tenant-management).

## Does the AI assistant have write access to my data?

Only in a limited, approval-gated way. The assistant is read-only by default; its SQL is restricted to `SELECT`/`WITH` queries, anything it creates (a saved query or dashboard) requires your explicit approval, and it can never delete. See [AI assistant](/agenteye/assistant).

## How is AgentEye different from Failproof AI Guards?

AgentEye is for **observing and improving** agents after the fact: tracing runs, scoring quality, finding failures, and alerting. Guards is a separate Failproof AI product focused on **policies and guardrails** that act on agent behavior. They're complementary; this documentation set covers AgentEye.
